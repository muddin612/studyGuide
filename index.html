New Jersey Notary Public Study Guide

Objective: Implements the provisions of P.L. 2021, c.179 and N.J.A.C. 17:50.

Goals:

  • Streamline the commissioning process.
  • Clarify and expand requirements for notarial acts.
  • Promote the use of new technologies for notarization.
  • Enhance transparency and accountability in notarial services.
  • Improve notarial service levels statewide.
  • Strengthen the reliability and integrity of notarial practices.

Key Terms:

  • Acknowledgment: A declaration that an individual has signed a record for the purpose stated in the record.
  • Electronic: Relating to technology having electrical, digital, magnetic, wireless, optical, electromagnetic, or similar capabilities.
  • Electronic Signature: An electronic symbol, sound, or process attached to, or logically associated with, a record and executed or adopted by an individual with the intent to sign the record.
  • Notarial Act: An act a notarial officer may perform under the laws of New Jersey, including taking acknowledgments, administering oaths, taking verifications on oath, witnessing signatures, certifying copies, and noting protests of negotiable instruments.
  • Notary Public: An individual commissioned by the State Treasurer to perform notarial acts.
  • Official Stamp: A physical image affixed to or embossed on a tangible record or an electronic image attached to, or logically associated with, an electronic record.
  • Verification on Oath or Affirmation: A declaration, made by an individual on oath or affirmation before a notarial officer, that a statement in a record is true.

Eligibility Criteria:

  • At least 18 years old.
  • Legal resident of New Jersey or have a place of employment in NJ.
  • Not disqualified to receive a commission under Chapter 5.

Scope of Authority:

  • Authorized to perform notarial duties throughout NJ.

Prohibited Acts:

  • Cannot notarize if the notary or their spouse/partner has a direct beneficial interest in the document.
  • Cannot advertise as a lawyer if not licensed as an attorney-at-law.
  • Cannot provide legal advice if not an attorney.

Application Procedures:

  • Apply online using the form prescribed by the State Treasurer.
  • Application must be endorsed by a member of the Legislature.
  • Non-refundable application fee of $25.00.

Initial Commission:

  • Complete a six-hour course of study approved by the State Treasurer.
  • Pass an examination prescribed by the State Treasurer.
  • Educational and testing requirements effective from July 2022.

Renewal:

  • Complete a three-hour continuing education course if previously completed the six-hour course and passed the exam.
  • Effective from July 2022.

Swearing-in:

  • Take an oath within three months of commission receipt before the county clerk where the notary resides or works.
  • The oath must be filed with the county clerk.

Commissioning of Nonresidents:

  • Nonresidents may apply if they maintain or are regularly employed in an office in NJ or perform duties remotely for a NJ business.
  • Must certify residence and employment details and file changes with the State Treasurer.

Name Change:

  • If a notary adopts a new name, they must file a statement under oath detailing the name change circumstances before signing any documents under the new name.

Reasons for Denial:

  • Failure to comply with P.L. 2021, c. 179 (N.J.S.A. 52:7-10 et seq.).
  • Fraudulent, dishonest, or deceitful misstatement or omission in the application.
  • Finding against, or admission of liability by, the notary in any legal or disciplinary action based on fraud or dishonesty.
  • Conviction of a crime of the second degree or above.
  • Failure to discharge any required duty.
  • Use of false or misleading advertising or representation.
  • Providing unauthorized legal services if not licensed as an attorney.

Notification and Hearing Process:

  • Written notice of denial, suspension, revocation, or limitation provided to the applicant or commission holder.
  • Details of violations and right to request a hearing within 20 days.
  • Hearing conducted according to the Administrative Procedure Act, and any final decision can be appealed to the Appellate Division of the Superior Court.

Certificates and Stamps:

  • Notarial acts must be evidenced by a certificate and stamped by the notary.
  • Certificate must be signed, dated, and include the jurisdiction and title of the notary.
  • Stamp includes the notary's name, title, and commission expiration date.
  • Notarial acts for tangible records require the certificate to be part of or attached to the record.
  • Electronic records require the certificate to be logically associated with the record.

Certificate Forms:

  • Short form certificates for various notarial acts are provided, including acknowledgments, verifications on oath, witnessing signatures, and certifying copies.

Journal Requirement:

  • Maintain a journal of all notarial acts.
  • Journal can be tangible (bound register) or electronic (tamper-evident).
  • Record date, time, type of act, names and addresses, method of identification, and fees charged.
  • Retain the journal for 10 years after the last notarial act.
  • Notify the State Treasurer if the journal is lost or stolen within 10 days.

Copy Certification:

  • Ensure the copy is a full, true, and accurate transcription or reproduction of the record.

Forms of Identification:

  • Personal Knowledge: Known through sufficient dealings.
  • Documentation:
    • Passport, driver’s license, or government-issued ID (current or expired within three years).
    • Verification of identity through a credible witness if necessary.

General Provisions:

  • Notary and individual must communicate in real-time using secure technology.
  • Identity proofing via methods like dynamic knowledge-based authentication or biometric verification.
  • Remote notarization must comply with New Jersey laws.

Requirements:

  • Notify the State Treasurer of intent to perform remote notarizations.
  • Confirm the identity of the individual using approved methods.
  • Create and retain an audio-visual recording of the notarization process for 10 years.

Remote Notarial Acts:

  • Remote notarial acts are deemed performed in New Jersey and governed by New Jersey law.
  • Ensure the record before the notarial officer is the same as the one the remotely located individual signed.

Taking an Acknowledgment When a Record is in the Possession of the Notary Public:

  • Display the record to the remotely located individual during the audio-visual session.

Performing a Notarial Act When a Record is Not Physically Present Before the Notary Public:

  • Remotely located individual signs the record and makes a declaration.
  • Send the record and declaration to the notarial officer within three days.
  • Notarial officer executes the notarial certificate after receiving the record.

Administering Oaths to Remotely Located Individuals:

  • Identify the individual with satisfactory forms of identification.
  • Create an audio-visual recording of the oath administration.
  • Retain the recording for 10 years.

General Provisions:

  • Use tamper-evident technologies for electronic notarizations.
  • Notify the State Treasurer before performing electronic notarizations.
  • Electronic signatures and stamps must be reliable and independently verifiable.

Requirements for Electronic Notarization:

  • Obtain satisfactory identification for the individual.
  • Complete an electronic certificate with an electronic signature and stamp.
  • Ensure the electronic signature and stamp are unique, verifiable, and tamper-evident.
  • Do not disclose access information for the electronic signature and stamp except when required by law.

Criteria for Refusal:

  • Doubt about the individual’s competence or voluntary signature.
  • Signature does not substantially conform to ID.
  • Physical appearance does not match the ID photo.
  • Refusal is prohibited by a State of New Jersey law.

Fee Structure:

  • $2.50 per act for standard services.
  • $15.00 for real estate transaction acknowledgments.
  • $25.00 for real estate financing acknowledgments.
